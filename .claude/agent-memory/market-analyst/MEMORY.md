## Regional Insights

### NCA — Nevada County, California (Regional Score: 11/20)

- Population peaked at 103,517 in 2021, now declining (~102k) — structural shrinkage
- DOM jumped from 57 to 71 days YoY (+25%) — buyer's market accelerating
- Wildfire insurance crisis: "moderate" risk areas reclassified to "very high"; FAIR Plan premiums up 15-40% with no ceiling; this LIMITS conventional buyer pool, making owner-carry structures more valuable (bypasses lender insurance requirements)
- Broadband expansion active: Bright Fiber approved for 1,941 households; Nevada County Last Mile Grants ongoing
- NV-49 Corridor infrastructure project in active construction phase 2026
- Adjacent Placer County had CA's highest net migration rate in 2024 (22.17 per 1k) — spill demand into western Nevada County
- Best opportunities: long-DOM car washes, storage, gas stations in Grass Valley/Penn Valley where insurance crisis keeps conventional buyers out

### CO — Colorado (Regional Score: 15/20)

- State hit 6M population (Jan 2026) but growth slowed to 0.4%; first net domestic outflow since 2004
- CU forecast: 17,500 new jobs in 2026, GDP +2.9% (above national)
- Best sub-zone: CO-FRONTRANGE (score 17/20)
  - Pueblo: 8-12% cap rates, $180k medians, EVRAZ steel expansion, rents +12% YoY
  - Colorado Springs: multifamily rents -7% YoY creating motivated sellers; defense/aerospace is stable employment anchor
- Denver industrial: vacancy 7.9-9.8% but construction starts at 2015 lows — tightening expected mid-2026
- Grand Junction: $200k-300k multifamily entry, 10-15% total returns — Western Slope value play
- Routt County: $5k/hire remote worker incentive — commercial tenant attraction tool

## Analysis Patterns

### Insurance Crisis as Owner-Finance Opportunity (NCA)

When insurance withdrawal limits conventional buyer pool, seller-carry notes
become the only viable financing mechanism. This creates:
- Motivated sellers (can't attract conventional buyers)
- Lower competition for assets
- Owner-carry note terms more negotiable (seller needs the deal too)
- Watch: gas stations and storage are less affected by residential fire insurance

### Distress Signals Observed in Pipeline

- DOM > 71 days in NCA = elevated motivation (market avg is 71, so anything above is outlier)
- DOM > 50 days in CO = moderate motivation (market avg 50-65 days)
- Multifamily in CO Springs with listed rents below $1,200/unit = below-market (stress signal)
- Industrial Denver with asking cap rate >7% = priced for vacancy, expect concession room

### Cross-Zone Decision Framework

1. Highest cap rate / lowest entry: Pueblo CO multifamily
2. Highest seller motivation: NCA long-DOM + insurance crisis
3. Best mid-term upside: Denver industrial small-bay (2026 tightening)
4. Best owner-finance negotiation environment: NCA (limited buyer competition) and CO Springs (seller stress)
