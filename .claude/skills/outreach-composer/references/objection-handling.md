# Objection Handling Reference

Common seller/broker objections and effective responses for owner-financed CRE deals.

## "We want all cash"

**Response**: "I completely understand. The reason I'm proposing owner financing
is that it actually benefits you in several ways. First, you avoid a large
tax hit from a lump-sum sale — an installment sale lets you spread the gain
over time. Second, you earn interest on the note, which is often better than
what you'd get reinvesting the cash. And third, we can close faster since
there's no bank underwriting. Would you be open to exploring the numbers?"

## "The price is firm"

**Response**: "I respect that, and your price is fair for the market. I'm not
looking to negotiate on price — I'm more focused on the terms. If we can
agree on financing structure that works for both of us, I'm prepared to
pay your asking price."

## "We need to think about it"

**Response**: "Of course, this is a big decision. I want you to feel
comfortable. Would it be helpful if I put together a brief summary
of what the deal would look like — monthly payments, interest, and
the tax benefits? That way you have something concrete to consider.
I'll follow up [day] to see where you're at."

## "We already have a buyer"

**Response**: "That's great. If that falls through for any reason, I'd love
to be your backup offer. Owner-financed deals don't have financing
contingencies, so there's no risk of the deal falling apart due to a
bank denial. Can I leave my info with you just in case?"

## "Why should we finance instead of selling outright?"

**Response**: "Three main reasons: (1) Tax efficiency — installment sales
under IRC Section 453 let you defer capital gains over the payment period
instead of paying it all in year one. (2) Monthly income — you receive
predictable payments, which is especially useful in retirement. (3) Higher
effective price — because you earn interest on the note, your total proceeds
often exceed what you'd net from a cash sale after taxes."

## "What if you stop paying?"

**Response**: "Great question. The property is the collateral — just like a bank
mortgage. If I default, the property reverts back to you, and you keep all
payments I've made. You're actually in a stronger position than a bank because
you already know the property. We'll record everything properly with title
insurance and a standard deed of trust."

## "We need a large down payment"

**Response**: "I understand you want security in the deal. Let's talk about what
number would make you comfortable. I'm flexible and want to structure
something that gives you confidence while making the deal work for both of us."
