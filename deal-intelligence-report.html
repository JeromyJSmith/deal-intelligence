Seller Intelligence Protocol • Confidential
The Intelligence Layer
Research First.
Then Call.
Every seller in this report has been profiled before a single call is made. We know their archetype, their pain signals, and their likely objections. The outreach that follows is calibrated to those findings — but the seller never knows we know. To them, we're just a buyer who showed up at the right time.
Layer 1 — Surface Intelligence
CREXI listing data, DOM, price history, financing signals, broker name, property type. Available in 60 seconds. Tells us WHAT the deal looks like.
Layer 2 — Deep Intelligence
County assessor records, ownership history, purchase price, entity structure, liens, court records, business registrations. Takes 30–60 minutes. Tells us WHO the seller really is.
Layer 3 — Behavioral Profiling
Archetype identification, pain signal mapping, resistance prediction, motivation scoring. Synthesizes Layers 1 and 2 into the Seller Pain Map on every deal file. Tells us HOW to approach.
◆ THE STEALTH PRINCIPLE
We know everything. We say almost nothing.
The outreach scripts in this report are informed by the Seller Pain Map on each deal — but they never reveal it. We don't mention days on market. We don't reference listing history. We don't use the seller's pain against them. We ask questions, we listen, and we let them tell us their story. The intelligence prepares us. The conversation closes the deal.

◆ THE 3-CALL CLOSE — Standard Sequence
Call 1
Contact & Qualify
Under 5 minutes. Confirm it's still available. Establish you're a real buyer. Drop one line about "working directly with sellers." Close by asking if there's a better time to talk about structure.
Call 2
Explore & Align
Listen 80%. Ask what their ideal exit looks like. Ask what timeline matters to them. Ask if they've thought about spreading payments versus a lump sum. Let them reveal the pain. Don't pitch.
Call 3
Propose & Structure
Come in with specific terms. Full asking price. Named interest rate. Named amortization. Named balloon. Give them two options — one slightly better for the buyer, one slightly better for the seller. Both are wins.

Layer 04 — Tax Intelligence
The Write-Off Most Buyers Never See
Short-term rentals with an average stay under 7 days are not classified as rental income under IRC §469. That changes everything. Combined with cost segregation and bonus depreciation, a single STR acquisition can generate $30K–$80K in Year 1 tax savings — cash in hand, before the first guest checks in.
Strategy A
STR Loophole
Average guest stay < 7 days → property treated as business, not rental. Losses flow through to W-2 income without REPS. No 750-hour requirement. The most powerful tax position in residential real estate.
Eligible ZIPs: Joshua Tree 92252 · Palm Springs 92262 · Denver 80203
Strategy B
Cost Segregation + Bonus Dep.
Cost seg study reclassifies 25–30% of building value as 5/15-year property. That portion qualifies for 60% bonus depreciation in Year 1 (2024 rate). Stacked with STR loophole = maximum deduction in Year 1.
Works on: Any STR, MHP, commercial with building value > 65% of purchase
Strategy C
1031 + OZ Exit
Stack as entry strategy: acquire via owner finance, operate STR for 5–7 years, 1031 into larger asset — or exit via Opportunity Zone fund for permanent deferral. 10-year OZ hold = $0 federal capital gains tax on appreciation.
OZ-eligible: Parts of Twentynine Palms, Coachella, Indio, NE Denver
