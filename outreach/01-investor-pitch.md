# Investor Pitch — Warm Contacts

Use these when reaching out to real estate people you already know (friends, past clients, networking contacts) who work with investors.

---

## TEXT MESSAGE (Casual / Texting Buddies)

> Hey man quick question — do your investors only do residential or are they open to commercial stuff with owner financing already built in? I found something that could be a serious acquisition pipeline in literally any state. Might not fit what you guys do but if it does... it's kind of insane. You got 10 min to chat?

### Variation — If They Work in a Specific State

> Hey [Name] — I know you guys work mostly in [State] but can your investors buy anywhere? I found a deal pipeline that's pulling cash-flowing assets with owner financing across all 50 states. If that's in their wheelhouse I think we should talk.

### Variation — If You Know They Do Commercial

> Yo I need to run something by you. What if I told you I found a way to source businesses and commercial properties where the seller is literally offering to finance the deal? Not cold calling, not hoping — they're already asking for it. Hit me back when you get a sec.

---

## EMAIL (Professional but Warm)

**Subject:** Quick question about your investors' buy box

Hey [Name],

Hope things are going well. I wanted to reach out because I've been doing some research on a deal sourcing angle that I think could be really valuable for the right investor group — but I want to make sure it's even relevant before I go deeper.

Here's what I can tell you:

I've built a repeatable method for finding commercial properties and operating businesses where the seller has already signaled they're open to owner financing. These aren't cold leads — these are motivated sellers who've made it clear they'll carry the note.

The kinds of deals surfacing are things like mobile home parks, storage facilities, retail centers, service businesses, hospitality — real cash-flowing assets, not speculative plays. Many of these sellers fit a very specific profile: they've owned and self-managed the asset for 10-20+ years and are ready to exit, but have strong financial reasons to structure the sale as seller-financed rather than a straight cash transaction.

A few high-level numbers from an initial scan:

- 1,400+ properties across 45+ states with owner financing signals
- Deals ranging from $50K to $32M with a concentration in the sweet spot
- Average days on market north of 400 — serious negotiating room
- Property types spanning housing, hospitality, fuel/convenience, storage, restaurants, industrial, and more

I've built a scoring and filtering system that can be tuned to whatever buy box your investors work with — geography, property type, price range, cap rate, you name it.

If this sounds like it could fit, I'd love to hop on a quick call and walk you through a few examples. No pressure — just think there's something real here.

Best,
Jero
