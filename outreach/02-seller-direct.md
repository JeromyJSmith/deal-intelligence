# Seller / Broker Direct Outreach — For Your Own Deals

Use these when YOU want to buy. These are for calling or emailing the broker/seller on a CREXI listing where they've already indicated owner financing.

The key: you're not asking them to do something weird. They ALREADY said they'd owner finance. You're just the guy actually taking them up on it.

---

## PHONE SCRIPT — Calling the Broker

> Hi, is this [Broker Name]? Hey, my name's Jero — I was looking at the [Property Name] listing on CREXI, the one in [City, State]. I see the seller's open to owner financing and I wanted to learn more about the terms they had in mind.
>
> I'm an active buyer looking for cash-flowing assets, and honestly, owner financing is exactly how I like to structure deals. It's a win-win — the seller gets monthly income without the tax hit, and I get to close without jumping through bank hoops.
>
> Can you tell me a little more about what the seller's looking for in terms of down payment, rate, and term?

### If They Ask About Your Experience

> I've been actively sourcing commercial deals across multiple states. I work with a team that specializes in identifying owner-financed opportunities and we move quickly when the numbers work. I'm not looking to waste anyone's time — if the deal pencils, I'm ready to go.

### If They Ask About Proof of Funds

> Happy to provide whatever the seller needs to feel comfortable. For owner-financed deals, the beauty is the property's cash flow covers the payments — so it's really about whether the numbers work for both sides. I can put together a package that shows my background and how I'd structure the acquisition.

## PHONE SCRIPT — Calling the Seller Directly

> Hi [Seller Name], my name's Jero. I came across your property listing — the [Property Name] in [City]. I understand you're open to owner financing, and honestly that's exactly what caught my eye.
>
> I know you've had this listed for a while and I respect the fact that you're not just looking to dump this thing. It sounds like you've put a lot of years into this property. If you're open to it, I'd love to talk about what a deal might look like where I make payments directly to you at a fair price.
>
> What would be the best way to continue this conversation?

### For a Retiring Seller (MHP, Storage, Small Business)

> Hi [Seller Name], my name's Jero. I saw your listing for the [Property Name] and I wanted to reach out personally. I know running a [property type] for [X] years takes a lot of work and it sounds like you've earned the right to step back.
>
> What I love about owner financing is it lets you retire on your terms — steady monthly income, no big tax event, and you don't have to 1031 into something bigger just to defer. You actually get to be done.
>
> I'd like to talk about making this work for both of us. What's the best time to connect?

## EMAIL — To Broker

**Subject:** Inquiry on [Property Name] — [City, State] (Owner Financing)

Hi [Broker Name],

I came across the [Property Name] listing on CREXI and I'm very interested. I see the seller is open to owner financing, which is exactly how I prefer to structure acquisitions.

I'm an active buyer focused on cash-flowing commercial assets. I appreciate deals where the financing structure benefits both sides — the seller receives consistent monthly income with favorable tax treatment, and the buyer can close efficiently without traditional bank underwriting.

I'd love to learn more about:
- The seller's preferred terms (down payment, rate, amortization, balloon)
- Current occupancy and NOI if available
- Any flexibility on the asking price given financing structure

I'm ready to move quickly when the deal makes sense. Would you have 15 minutes this week for a quick call?

Best regards,
Jero
[Phone Number]

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **They already said owner financing.** You're not pitching something crazy — you're responding to their offer.
2. **Acknowledge their years of work.** Especially for retiring sellers. They want to know you respect what they built.
3. **Speak their language.** Tax benefits, monthly income, smooth transition — that's what they care about.
4. **Don't oversell yourself.** Be confident but not cocky. Let the deal structure speak for itself.
5. **Move fast.** These sellers have been waiting. Responsiveness = credibility.
