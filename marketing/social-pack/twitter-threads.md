## THREAD 04 — Owner Finance Explained

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
"Owner financing" sounds like a niche creative finance trick.

It's actually the most rational deal structure in CRE right now. Here's why. 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Let's say you bought a mobile home park in 2001 for $500K.

It's now worth $3M.

You want to sell.

If you sell for cash: capital gains tax on $2.5M. Due in April. Massive check to the IRS.

**Tweet 3:**
If you carry the note (seller financing):

You receive payments over 20-30 years.

You pay capital gains tax ONLY as you receive principal.

Tax-deferred income stream. For decades.

For a retiring operator, this isn't "creative" — it's mathematically obvious.

**Tweet 4:**
For the buyer:

You get a loan below bank rates (sellers offer 5-7% vs. current bank rates of 7-9%+)

No bank underwriting. No appraisal delays. No DSCR requirements.

Close in weeks, not months.

**Tweet 5:**
The structure we use:

Purchase price: [Asking or negotiated]
Down: 10-20%
Seller note: 80-90% of purchase price
Rate: 5-7%
Amortization: 20-30 years
Balloon: 5-10 years

Monthly payment < NOI = cash flow from day one.

**Tweet 6:**
Owner financing isn't a last resort.

It's the optimal structure for a specific type of seller at a specific stage of their lifecycle.

We find those sellers.

We show up with the right structure.

And we close.
