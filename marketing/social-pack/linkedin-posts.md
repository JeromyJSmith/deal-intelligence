## POST 05 — Why Banks Are Your Competitive Advantage

The bank said no.

That's actually good news.

Here's the part most investors miss:

Banks refuse to finance:
- Special purpose properties (gas stations, car washes, self-storage)
- Operating businesses with real estate attached
- Properties with environmental conditions
- Any deal below $1M that's "too small" for their loan minimums

When a bank says no, 90% of would-be buyers disappear.

The seller is now dealing with a radically smaller buyer pool.

We specialize in exactly these deals. Not because we can't find conventional deals — because the bankable deals have the most competition.

The unlendable deal is the best deal.

## POST 07 — The Owner-Finance Thesis (Educational)

A seller who owns a property outright has a problem.

If they sell for cash: they pay capital gains tax. Immediately.

If they carry the note (seller financing): they pay capital gains tax over time, as they receive payments. Tax-deferred income stream.

For a seller who bought a mobile home park 25 years ago for $300K and is now selling for $2M... the tax difference is life-changing.

That's why retiring operators LOVE owner financing.

We find them.
We structure the deal so the tax benefit is clear.
We show up with a 30-year amortization at 6% — below bank rate for the seller, and they're getting paid for 30 years instead of a tax bill in April.

Everybody wins.

That's the deal structure we use on every acquisition.

## POST 09 — The Seller Archetypes

Not every motivated seller is the same.

After 6 years of this, I've identified 5 archetypes:

**The Retiring Operator** — Owned and self-managed the asset for 20+ years. Wants income without the headache. Responds to: "You've built something real. Let's structure an exit that pays you every month."

**The Exhausted Holder** — Tried to sell multiple times. Nothing stuck. Energy is low, patience is gone. Responds to: "I can move fast and I won't waste your time."

**The Failed Speculator** — Bought as an investment, it didn't perform. Underwater on their original thesis. Responds to: "This doesn't have to be a loss. Here's a structure that gets you whole."

**The Portfolio Exit** — Liquidating a collection of assets. Just wants clean, simple, repeatable closes. Responds to: "Let me buy the rest of your portfolio too."

**The Distressed Owner** — Liens, code violations, deferred maintenance, personal financial stress. Responds to: speed, certainty, no contingencies.

Each one gets a different script. Same intelligence system, different human conversation.
