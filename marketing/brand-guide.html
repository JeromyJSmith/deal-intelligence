Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the "D" glyph around the mark. Never crowd the logo.
Do
  • Use on navy, white, teal, or dark image backgrounds
  • Maintain the teal/navy color relationship of the mark
  • Use the wordmark + mark together whenever space allows
  • Minimum mark size: 24px height
  • Use Playfair Display 700 or 900 weight for wordmark
Don't
  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or distort the mark
  • Use on busy photographic backgrounds without overlay
  • Use colors outside the brand palette for the mark
  • Add drop shadows, gradients, or effects to the mark
  • Use a font other than Playfair Display for "DI"

04–05 — Voice & Visual
Brand Voice
Voice & Tone
Deal Intelligence sounds like a smart friend who knows the real estate game. Specific, confident, never salesy. The data does the selling — we just present it cleanly.
Specific Confident Direct Data-driven Authoritative Not salesy Not hypey Not vague
DO — Sound Like This
  • "437 days on market. That's not a warning sign. That's leverage."
  • "Score: 95/100. 404 days on market. Confirmed seller financing."
  • "3,000 noise listings become 4 high-conviction opportunities."
  • "The seller has been waiting 13 months for the right buyer."
  • "Kalshi shows 71% probability of a rate cut. The window is closing."
  • "The bank said no. That's good news — 90% of buyers just left."
DON'T — Avoid This
  • "Unlock the secret to finding amazing deals!"
  • "Our AI will crush the competition for you."
  • "This is a game-changer for real estate investors."
  • "Massive ROI potential in our pipeline right now!"
  • "We use cutting-edge AI technology to find deals."
  • "Trust us — we've seen it all in this market."
Never Use These Words
guru secret hack crush it game-changer AI CREXI passive income get rich guaranteed returns amazing unlock massive synergy

07 — Do's & Don'ts
Quick Reference
Do's & Don'ts
A summary of all brand rules for rapid review before publishing any content under the Deal Intelligence name.
✓ DO
→
Use specific numbers (437 DOM, 95/100, $14.1M). Vagueness kills credibility.
→
Lead with the insight or data point, not the pitch. Let the number do the selling.
→
Use "multi-agent pipeline" or "intelligence system" — not "AI" alone.
→
Blur/redact proprietary deal details on all public-facing materials.
→
Use navy, white, or teal backgrounds. Dark + teal = the visual signature.
→
Use Playfair Display for all headlines and the DI mark.
→
Alternate content pillars: data → deal → thesis → system. Never two data posts in a row.
→
Include a CTA on every post: "DM SCOPE", "DM PIPELINE", or email.
→
Mention Kalshi, Polymarket, Parcl by name (they're public). Never mention CREXI.
→
Reference 6 years of iteration as proof of depth, not just "experience."
✗ DON'T
→
Never mention CREXI by name in any public content. That's our source, not public knowledge.
→
Never promise returns, yield guarantees, or specific deal outcomes to any audience.
→
Never say "AI" without context. Say "multi-agent pipeline" or "intelligence system."
→
Never reveal the exact 8 scoring signals, CREXI URL parameters, or full outreach scripts.
→
Never sound like a course sale or coaching pitch. This is a B2B service, not education.
→
Never use stock photos of handshakes, skylines, or people in suits.
→
Never use words: guru, secret, hack, crush it, game-changer, passive income, guaranteed.
→
Never post seller names, specific addresses, or broker contact info publicly.
→
Never use off-brand colors (especially bright greens, blues, or reds not in the palette).
→
Never redesign the DI mark or wordmark. The mark is fixed. Use it as-is.
