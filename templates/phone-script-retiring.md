# Phone Script: Retiring Owner

## Opening

"Hi {BROKER_NAME}, my name is {BUYER_NAME}. I'm calling about {PROPERTY_NAME} on {STREET}. I've been following this listing and I'm very interested."

## Build Rapport

"I can see this property has been well-maintained over the years. How long has the current owner had it?"

*(Listen — let them talk about the property and owner)*

## Transition to Financing

"That's impressive — {YEARS} years of ownership is a real testament to the quality of this asset. I'm curious — is the owner open to seller financing? I'd like to propose a purchase at the asking price of {PRICE} with owner financing."

## Handle Response

**If positive**: "Great! Here's what I'm thinking — {DOWN_PAYMENT}% down, with monthly payments amortized over 25 years at {RATE}%. The owner gets steady monthly income, great tax treatment on the installment sale, and doesn't have to worry about a 1031 exchange. What does the owner think about that structure?"

**If hesitant**: "I understand. The reason I bring it up is that for a retiring owner, an installment sale can be really beneficial. They avoid the big tax hit of a lump-sum sale, they get monthly passive income, and they don't need to find another property for a 1031 exchange. Would the owner at least be open to a conversation about it?"

**If no**: "I appreciate your honesty. If things change or if the current marketing approach isn't getting the results the owner wants, I'd love to be kept in mind. Can I send you my contact info?"

## Close

"What would be the best next step? I can send over a letter of intent this week if the owner is interested in exploring this."

## Key Points to Remember

- Acknowledge the owner's years of work
- Frame owner financing as THEIR benefit (tax, income, simplicity)
- Don't negotiate price — negotiate terms
- Be patient — this may take multiple touchpoints
- Always be respectful of the retirement decision
