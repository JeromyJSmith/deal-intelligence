The Q-Score Formula

Liquidity rewards are distributed daily based on Q-score:

S(v, s) = ((v - s) / v)² × b

v = market's rewardsMaxSpread  |  s = your spread distance from midpoint  |  b = order size

Quadratic function — tighter spreads are rewarded non-linearly. An order at the midpoint scores perfectly; one at the edge scores near zero. Two-sided liquidity earns minimum of both sides' scores. Single-sided gets divided by 3.

The "French Whale" Case Study — $80M Proof of Concept

One entity placed $45M+ in Trump bets across 4 accounts (Fredi9999, Theo4, PrincessCaro, Michie), skewing Polymarket odds. Media widely reported these elevated odds as evidence Trump was the frontrunner. Trump won. The whale profited $80M+.

The unresolved question: Did elevated Polymarket odds influence undecided voters, donors, or media coverage? No definitive causal study exists — but the circumstantial chain is documented by the Atlantic Council, WSJ, and CBS 60 Minutes.

Behavioral Biases Traders Exhibit

  • Favorite-Longshot Bias: Longshots overbet, favorites underbet (5% chance "feels" like 10-15%)
  • Anchoring: Prior probability estimates create sticky priors that traders update from insufficiently
  • Herding: Whale trades create sustained price movements amplified by traders who follow the price
  • Optimism Bias: Corporate markets show systematic overestimation of project completion
  • Wishful Thinking: Political markets: supporters trade on hope, not true probability estimates
  • Loss Aversion: Participants refuse to close losing positions, distorting market-level dynamics
"When participants have access to prior probability estimates, they update insufficiently and remain anchored to the initial value." — ScienceDirect study on anchoring in experimental markets

The Five Viral Triggers

  1. Existential stakes — Outcomes that genuinely matter at societal level (elections, wars)
  2. Real-time feedback loop — When markets move faster than media, they become the story
  3. Near 50/50 probability — Maximum uncertainty = maximum engagement
  4. Short resolution window — Days/hours to resolution creates urgency
  5. Cross-tribal appeal — Both sides believe they're right → both sides bet

Partition Dependence: Multi-outcome markets suffer from systematic mispricing — participants anchor on 1/N probability regardless of actual distribution. Design lever: Create chains of binary markets instead of single multi-outcome markets to avoid this bias and create more cross-promotion opportunities.

  • Complementary Series: Propose related markets forming a logical chain. Arbitrageurs enforce price consistency, creating cross-market volume.
